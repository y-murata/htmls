We will continue to improve corporate value so that we may provide customers with an enriched and flexible lifestyle.

With the aging of society, these are ever more people wishing for lifelong health and beauty. Customer needs have also diversified. We have grown by pursuing the creation of stores that are familiar to and loved by all the people throughout the regions based on our mottos "kindness first" and "Trust first" and support our customer's efforts to create healthy and enriched lives. Expectations will become higher than ever for conveniently located drugstores where anyone can easily engage in consultations and enjoy improved awareness of self-medication by which they can manage their own health.

In the drugstore sector, the operating environment remained challenging, with competition to open stores and reduce prices continuing to intensify and the pace of consolidation and realignment accelerating as companies sought to remain competitive.

In the fiscal year ending May 15, 2015, the TSURUHA Group continued to offer a high level of customer service mainly through advice-based sales, and implemented a number of strategic business initiatives. Specifically, in addition to focusing on advice-based sales of high value-added products that satisfy customer needs, the Group actively developed its range of high-quality low-price private brand products marketed under the “M’s one” name, and pushed ahead with store renovations aimed at improving convenience for customers by introducing food products. The Group also expanded the use of new sales promotion methods to replace sales flyer distribution.

In store openings, the Group targeted specific areas for multiple store openings as part of its area dominance store-opening strategy and implemented a scrap and build approach for existing stores. In the fiscal year under review, the Group opened 100 new stores and closed 34. Also, effective March 1, 2015, our subsidiary HEARTY WANTS Co., Ltd. acquired five stores from KYOEI PHARMACY CO., LTD. As a result, the number of directly-managed stores totaled 1,383 as of the year ended May 15, 2015. Regarding our stores in Thailand, the Group opened 13 new stores and closed five, resulting in a total of 23 stores at the end of the fiscal period.

As a result of the above, for the fiscal year under review, TSURUHA Holdings reported net sales of ¥440,427 million (up 13.4% year on year) and operating income of ¥26,905 million (up 11.6%). Net income was ¥17,210 million (up 18.2%).

We will continue to strengthen our reliance-based ties with all people nationwide, so we sincerely hope that we will continue to receive your support as Japan's number one drug store chain.

Masashi Horikawa
President and Representative Director

Corporate Profile

The Bank of Iwate, established in 1932, as a key financial institution in Iwate Prefecture, the largest prefecture on Japan's main island of Honshu. The prefecture, which is the Bank's business base, boasts a wide range of activity, from traditional industries to advanced technology. The Bank is committed to offering all manner of products and services with an eye to the new financial age, and is boldly stepping forward together with Iwate Prefecture into the 21st century.

The Bank of Iwate group, consisting of the Bank itself, one subsidiary, and three affiliated companies as of the end of March 2014, is engaged in offering a wide variety of financial services centered on the banking business, including leasing and credit cards as well as clerical work outsourcing for the Bank.

As of the end of March 2014, the Bank of Iwate group had total assets of ¥3,517 billion (US$34,170 million) and total deposits of ¥3,284 billion (US$31,912 million).

The Bank of Iwate's head office,located in the heart of Morioka City,functions as one of Iwate Prefecture'smain financial centers.
In 1994, the building of the old Nakanohashi Branch(*) in Morioka City(the former head office) was designated an important cultural property.
(* The Nakanohashi Branch is doing business at the new store which will adjoin from August,2012.)
